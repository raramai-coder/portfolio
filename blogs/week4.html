What is Semantic Markup

Semantic Markup is writing HTML in such a way that the semantic meaning of content is prioritised over the appearance. Previously, HTML tags were chosen based on how they would appear in the browser, for example an h1 or h2 tag might be chosen because of its sizing and appearance. But now it is best to choose an h1 or h2 tag based on priority and hierarchy, and then do the styling in CSS. Essentially separating the appearance from the structure in code.

Semantic Markup takes into consideration how both humans and machine will interact with the information on the page. Helping developers, browsers, web crawlers and other devices distinguish between different kinds of information and its importance.

Semantic elements clearly define the content encapsulated for example <form>, <article>, <nav>etc. Unlike non-semantic elements such as <div> and <span> that give no clue as to the kind of information contained in them. It is highly encouraged to be as semantic as possible.

Why does it matter?

There are three main reasons why semantic markup is important:

1.SEO

Search engines weigh keywords and make rankings based on the kind of information presented, its importance based on hierarchy. Semantics helps to define all of that to the search engine. Successfully writing your HTML as semantic as possible will lead to your webpage being ranked favourably.

2.Accessibility

People who are differently abled will be able to experience your webpage efficiently, through the use of assisting tools if your page is marked up correctly. They can customize their experience to best suit them and have their tools present your webpage to them in the way that was intended.

3.Maintenance

Code that is semantic is clean and much easier to read, so this makes it easy to maintain and easy to collaborate on. Future collaborators will also find it easy to understand and hence adjust and maintain the code. Appearance is also much easier to customize, modify and update through CSS when semantic markup is used. Customising the semantic elements becomes much more efficient.
