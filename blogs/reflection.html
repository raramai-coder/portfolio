Critical Reflection of Work

This reflection is split into two parts: reflection of the website development and reflection of my theoretical engagement of course content. For the reflection of my web development I used 7 key indicators of best practice in web development as laid out by www.visualobjects.com (Wess,2021).

Web Development

Planning

It is important for a developer to program with an end goal in mind and a roadmap on how to get there in order to yield the best results (Wess,2021). Making the wireframes at the beginning of the semester really helped in giving me an end goal and a design vision that made sure that as I was working, I could stay focused. As I reflect, I am made into a religious proponent of making wireframes at the start of any development project.

Looking back, I see how it saved me time as I was working because I no longer had to think about the creative or design elements of the website was structured because that was already done in the beginning. And even if I had new pages or features to add to the website that weren’t initially in the wireframes, I found that I had a guiding design vision that made it easy to be creative and to follow the vision to design the new pages or features as quickly as possible.

Working in the order of how the lessons were structured really helped me with the process of development as a first-time web developer. Starting with the HTML then doing the CSS and finally adding JavaScript, this helped with me developing my understanding for how the web works on a technical level as well as working in a way that was reflexive of increasing levels of complexity.

In future however I would much prefer to first get the skeleton of the website done using html and then working page by page to add the complete functionality (this means doing CSS and JavaScript at the same time) until that page is done. I prefer to work in increments of creating a Minimum Viable Product at the end of each increment. This keeps me motivated and makes me feel less overwhelmed because at the end of each increment I feel as though I have something tangible to show for my work and that goes a long way in helping me to not feel overwhelmed by how much work I still have to do.

It also makes user testing much easier because now I can test the Minimum Viable Products I have created at each increment as I go and get a better understanding of how the user is interacting and experiencing my website.

Accesibility

I did not begin with accessibility (in terms of UI and UX) at the forefront of my mind when I first began designing and programming my website. It was only midway after the lectures for UI/UX were released that this became a consideration of mine. In future I would like to have accessibility as a consideration at the very beginning of the project. That way I don’t have to go back and make the website more accessible or add accessibility features, because it would have been accessible from the get-go.

According to WebAIM Home page complexity has become an increasing problem in web development (WebAIM: The WebAIM Million – The 2022 report on the accessibility of the top 1,000,000 home pages, 2022). My home page is quite complex because of the scroll mechanism added. I realise that if a user does not have access to a mouse or their trackpad is not working as should be, the website will appear glitchy or even static to them.

In future I would like to adjust the homepage to make it more accessible and give users an alternative way to move through the carousel.

I also need to work on having alt text for my images. Since I am showcasing my work as a photographer, I find it of the utmost importance that all information about the images on my website is properly conveyed to the user.

I do feel however that my use of semantic mark up has been good and helps with the accessibility of my website in terms of communicating with the browser and its crawlers. This is because of the fact that from the first html lesson there was emphasis put on proper semantic markup and so it was always at the front of my mind throughout development.

User Experience

I wish I had gotten user testing data in order to be able to more accurately reflect on the user experience of my website. Because I can say I feel that the website has good user experience but that is clouded by the fact that as the designer and developer of the website I know how I want the website to be used and what I want to communicate so to me it is clear. But the real measure comes from whether someone who is engaging with the website for the first time is able to understand what I am trying to communicate and engage with the website how I intended.

Another issue is that because it was my first-time doing web development I focused and programming features and functionality. But in future, now that I have the experience and an idea of how to accomplish things in web development, I would like to more critically and actively think about how the user will interact with my webpages.

Essentially the ‘story’ of each webpage, where I want the interaction to begin and how it must flow. Making conscious the intention of how I want the user to interact with my webpages. This way it’s not by mistake that the thing that grabs my user’s attention is the heading instead of the image below it, but that that has a purpose and a reason behind it.

Conventions

For the most part I feel that I followed conventions and was not too experimental with my design. And I began wireframing from other portfolio websites that I catalogued and studied. So this helped me stick to convention and not deviate too much from standards.

Code Conciseness and Usefulness

I kept my code concise by only writing code for html and CSS that was necessary for the functionality I wanted to implement. In terms of JavaScript, I was conscious to not have any repeated lines of code that could simply be put into a function. The only time I had to repeated lines of code was for the two navigation functions because I realised that GitHub pages only works with relative linking and that my navigation function needed to be adjusted for whether or not a page was inside a different folder relative to the page it was trying to navigate to.

Compatibility Across Multiple Devices

I did not wireframe for mobile version of my website, and it was not consciously programmed for as I developed. For example, the programming for the mediaquery of what to when the webpage is resized to be smaller in width would help with how users experience the website on mobile, but this was done with webpages on a computer in mind and only coincidentally works for mobile as well.

Seeing as how mobile users are rapidly increasing and projected to increase across the globe (Smartphone users 2026 | Statista, 2022), it is of the utmost importance that I design and program with these users in mind as well. Going forward I would like to work on actively designing the app for the mobile user experience.

Theoretical

In the beginning I found that I approached the critical writings in a more informal way, so I did not reference properly and do my citations. But now I understand that that is something I need to do and include in my writings.

My own research was also something I did not do in the beginning, but I understand the value of getting information from other sources and being able to link that to the reading. It shows a cognitive process of understanding that is deeper than simply knowing what the author was trying to say but also being able to contextualise in the broader field of understanding that exists outside of that single source.

Another element of deeper understanding is being able to bring my own voice to the topics while there are writings where I feel I did this well, I also know that I struggled to do this with readings that I did not find particularly interesting or engaging.

So, I found that understanding the concepts expressed in the readings was not an issue for me, but making and conveying the depth of this understanding through the cognitive processes of linking and criticality of own voice are what I need to work on.

Lastly, I personally feel as though I gained a lot through the theoretical component of this course, but I did not sit with this theory and apply it to how I designed and developed my website, again owing to the fact that I was a beginner and was focused on making functionality work. But going forward I would like my design to reflect my decolonial ideas and intentions and to actively work to combat the problems of the web discussed in this course.

Self-Assesment

The self-assessment was quite difficult for me. I found that it was hard to calibrate my performance and give it a mark. Not because I am unable to be critical of my own work (this is something I am quite comfortable with) but because I was subconsciously not simply being critical of my work but trying gauge how the marker would view my work and then basing my marks on that.

This of course wad not a lovely experience and I struggle to see how in future I might be able to do it without subconsciously feeling like I am getting marked for how well I can guess the marker will feel about my work. I will of course try to shift from this mindset best as I can to one that is more focused on self-reflection that I way I can extract more value from the process.

Lastly, I feel my imposter syndrome really shines through in the self-assessment because there are parts where I feel I might have been to harsh on myself and not realised the true value of the work I did.
